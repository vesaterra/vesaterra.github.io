How much will precipitation departures change by 2030?

Description of Dataset >

Dataset title: Precipitation Change in Canada – Annual average precipitation departures from 1961–1990 reference value, Canada - Data table
Dataset link: https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/38145e6c-4184-43b1-afe1-c668a98ab56e/resource/5eadb6ec-4c17-4fe6-82b8-ec261fe73b0a?inner_span=True

This dataset contains precipitation departures ( in percentages ) throughout Canada from 1948 to 2014.

What are precipitation departures?

Precipitation departures are departures from the AVERAGE precipitation of the region. This metric indicates how much precipitation has deviated ( in percentage in this observation ) from the average precipitation value, which helps in understanding long-term trends.

What concerns us with precipitation departures?

Climate change has the ability to alter weather patterns and cycles, resulting in abnormally low or high precipitation.

Too much precipitation causes floods, ruins crops, and hindrances in society ( no internet, electricity, etc. ). Too little precipitation can lead to droughts, where food and water supply are drastically affected.
